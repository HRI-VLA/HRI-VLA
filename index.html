HRI-VLA: Facilitating Human-in-Loop Tasks by Vision-Language-Action Models

Anonymous CORL submission
Paper ID 327

Abstract

Human-robot interaction (HRI) plays a crucial role in enabling robots to support human production and daily life, yet it remains a formidable challenge. Significant advancements have been achieved through the design of cascaded finite-state machine systems composed of multiple modules, or by acquiring policies via reinforcement learning. However, the former approach becomes exponentially intractable when addressing complex, long-horizon tasks, while the latter suffers from the high cost of exploration involving human interaction or the need to construct intricate simulation environments---both of which risk the sim-to-real gap. This paper presents HRI-VLA, a framework that facilitates HRI through vision-language-action (VLA) models. We introduce the anchoring loop paradigm, a data collection strategy designed to enhance recording efficiency and streamline model training for HRI patterns. Experimental results show that HRI-VLA generalizes well across multiple VLA models and enables robots to engage in natural, seamless interactions with humans using only 20-shot finetuning.
